Table of Contents 1. Overview Importance of Water Quality This year is called the Year of Water Action. Governor Mark Dayton[Governor of Minnesota,2016] has begun an initiative aimed at “urging businesses, the agricultural industry, outdoor enthusiasts, communities and families to take action to conserve and improve water quality.” We are members of the Minnesota community and understand the importance of being informed about our state’s resources. Governor Dayton is dedicated to finding solutions for the conditions of the Minnesota lakes where at this time, 40% of Minnesota’s waters are considered to be polluted. Problem Statement To get a Understanding of how water lake Quality in Minnesota has been Performing. It is Also to understand how other Social data like property value, agriculture or Residents have Affected to water quality or vice versa

Objective and Scope of the Project Minnesota Water Quality and its impact in the Community. These Following Questions have been put Forwarded by the Minnesota Water Activists and Water Departments.

Main Questions:

a)How does property value influence water quality?

b)What property characteristics influence water quality the most?

c)Have changes in land use or land development over time positively or adversely impacted water quality?

d)What lakes tend to have the best / worst water quality?

e)Are the lakes with the best / worst water quality consistent over time? Data Sources http://minneanalytics.org/minnemudac/data/

Data Questions

Questions: Property Data Set

Property ownership is an investment and the hope is that your investment will gain value over time. What locations have seen the most substantial increase / decrease in property values over time? The type of dwelling or type of property owner (i.e. public, commercial, residual, etc.) may adversely impact water quality. What locations have seen the most change or growth in these characteristics over time? Local and county governments are often responsible for developing policies and regulating land development. What locations have seen the most change in land development over time? What variations exist in how land is being developed across various local and county governments?

Questions: Property Data Set

Property that is contiguous to a lake, i.e. lake front property, is likely to influence its value. What is the relationship between proximity to water and property value? What effect does location have on this relationship? Does time influence this relationship? What relationships exist between various property characteristics (i.e. dwelling type, ownership type, etc.) and proximity to water? Have these relationships changed over time? Do the policies / regulations of local or county governments appear to influence these relationships? Does proximity to water appear to influence how land is being developed over time? Does the amount of influence vary across local or county governments? Data Quality The data Was really huge for a 4GB memory Laptop. It was really a Messy Data, as we were expecting it to be. The lower the phosphorus level, the higher the grade. This is not a perfect correlation, but strong enough that we conclude that phosphorus is the main component to determine a body of water’s quality. Four of these top performing lakes are in the same watersheds, called the Lower St. Croix River Watershed. This leads us to believe that phosphorus and secci levels are consistent between the lakes in the watershed. indicating Eutrophication.This Makes Sense because phosphorus is the nutrient that most influences the growth of algae in lakes. Excess algae in lake water can cause a disturbance to the normal equilibrium of the aquatic ecosystem. As algae plants die and decompose, bacteria consume oxygen that is dissolved in the water. Increased algal growth can lead to a decline in oxygen levels, accelerating the decline of water quality/ declining water clarity, and decreasing transparency. Elements of land use and land development that impact water quality The value of a property adjacent to lakes, bays, and rivers reflects the overall quality of the water and area resources.Below is the Visualization of the Agricultural Preserve land nearby the lakes.
